UX Case Study

Emotions at Play: Designing Child-Centered AI for Emotional Learning

A participatory UX research and design project exploring how AI and tangible play can foster emotional intelligence in children. I led the full design cycle — from generative research and prototyping in Figma to iterative testing with real users.

Problem Statement

  1. State of the World: Play is a natural foundation for children’s emotional growth. Studies show that when children build stories, games, or characters, they externalize emotions, negotiate meaning, and develop empathy:contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}. Yet digital emotional-learning tools often remain adult-centered, focusing on structured skill training instead of spontaneous, child-led exploration.
  2. The Big BUT: Despite evidence that play transforms difficult emotions into manageable experiences, there is little research on how children themselves use playful and technological tools to express and reinterpret emotions collectively. This gap limits how we design emotionally intelligent systems for youth in an age of pervasive AI and screen time.
  3. Therefore, We Did: This study explored how children engage with AI-assisted and tangible tools to express, share, and reinterpret emotions through play. The goal was to design and evaluate the AI Emotional Sticker Tool — a hybrid interface that combines storytelling, empathy games, and AI image generation — to understand how technology can serve as a campfire for emotional learning.

Research outcome: Submitted as a full paper to CHI 2026, “Emotions at Play: Children’s Emotional Learning with AI and Tangible Tools.”

Design Philosophy

My UX philosophy emphasizes emotional intelligence, participatory design, and play as drivers of creativity and empathy. Grounded in sociocultural theory and constructivism, I view children not as end-users but as co-designers who bring emotional insight into the design process.

The goal was not only to design for emotional learning, but to design through it—creating tools that spark curiosity, agency, and shared reflection. This project treats technology, especially AI, as a reflective partner rather than an evaluator.

Discover · Play-Based Emotional Expression (PD1 + PD2)

PD1 · Storytelling Dice Game

Using dice inspired by Pixar’s Inside Out, children created stories around emotions such as anger, sadness, or joy. The activity revealed that difficult emotions were often the most fun to explore — curiosity became a gateway for emotional expression:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}.

PD2 · Empathy Game Design

Children co-designed empathy games (“You Are Not Alone”) using cards, dice, and wheels. By recognizing shared emotions, they turned vulnerability into social play, discovering that stickers and visual tokens were their favorite medium for expressing and sharing feelings:contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}.

Insight → Stickers emerged as playful emotional artifacts — visual “campfires” around which conversation and empathy formed.

Design & Develop · AI Emotional Sticker Tool

Building on PD insights, I prototyped the AI Emotional Sticker Tool in Figma, designed for self and peer reflection. The tool allows children to describe emotions, generate AI stickers, and customize them before printing.

Evaluate · User Testing & Iteration

During testing, children interacted with the AI tool individually and in pairs, creating stickers for both themselves and their peers. Humor and imperfection fueled engagement — children laughed at AI’s “mistakes,” leading to deeper emotional reflection and empathy.

Insight → Fun and imperfection lowered emotional barriers and encouraged collective reflection.

Conclusion · Campfire Model & Playful Learning

This project shows that emotional learning happens most effectively around shared, playful artifacts — what I call the Campfire Model of Emotional Design. Like a campfire, these tools draw children together to share stories, laugh, and reinterpret emotions collectively:contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}.

  • Curiosity – Playful uncertainty (dice, AI randomness) sparks emotional exploration.
  • Ownership – Children co-create and adapt rules, claiming voice and authorship:contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}.
  • Enjoyment – Humor transforms vulnerability into connection and empathy:contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}.

Together, these principles frame Playful Learning for Emotional UX — where emotional design is not about teaching correct feelings, but about cultivating environments that make emotions visible, social, and fun to explore.

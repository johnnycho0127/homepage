UX Case Study

Emotions at Play: Designing Child-Centered AI for Emotional Learning

This project explores how AI and tangible tools can foster children’s emotional learning through play. I led the end-to-end UX process—user research, interaction design, and prototyping—grounded in participatory design with 27 children (ages 6–12). The outcome was the AI Emotional Sticker Tool, a hybrid digital–physical interface that lets children express, share, and reinterpret emotions together.

Design Philosophy

My UX philosophy draws from human-centered design and pedagogies of play: design should empower curiosity, agency, and shared reflection. In emotionally sensitive contexts, usability alone is not enough—interfaces must cultivate empathy, safety, and co-experience.

I view design as collaborative inquiry—each iteration reveals how users think, feel, and make sense of tools. The process follows participatory design (PD) methodology, treating children as design partners rather than test subjects. This aligns with constructivist learning principles, where meaning emerges through play.

UX Methodology

  1. Discovery & Problem Framing – Literature review and initial observation of emotional play contexts revealed that existing SEL tools were too instructional and lacked child agency. I defined the challenge: “How might we design playful, emotionally intelligent tools that support empathy and reflection?”
  2. Participatory Research (PD1–PD4) – Conducted four design sprints combining storytelling, empathy game design, and AI-assisted emotional reflection:contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}. This allowed me to map emotional expression, peer collaboration, and AI interaction patterns.
  3. Prototyping – Designed low-fidelity sketches and Figma wireframes for the AI Emotional Sticker Tool. Iterated on feedback around tone, personalization, and playfulness.
  4. User Evaluation – Tested with children using think-aloud and peer reflection methods. Analyzed data via inductive thematic coding to identify patterns of engagement and emotional literacy:contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}.

Design Process and Artifacts

I moved through progressive fidelity from tangible to digital: first, paper-based emotion cards and dice; then mid-fidelity wireframes; and finally, an interactive prototype in Figma. The AI Sticker Tool was designed as a child-safe, editable AI interface that lets users generate, customize, and print emotional stickers.

User Research & Insights

Through four PD sessions, children engaged in narrative play, collaborative empathy design, and AI critique. Their feedback directly informed interaction changes.

  • Preferred tangible and flexible tools over fixed screens.
  • Mocked AI responses that felt inauthentic—preferring co-creation.
  • Expressed emotional insight when comparing self vs. peer-generated stickers.

Technical & Interaction Design

The prototype integrates AI-generated visuals using prompt engineering principles. Children’s text inputs (“I felt sad when...”) are processed into AI image prompts that emphasize warmth, humor, and empathy. To preserve emotional safety, all prompts are filtered for age-appropriate tone, and outputs are editable.

The interface design followed three guiding heuristics:

  • Transparency: Explain what the AI does (“The AI is creating a friendly image based on your story”).
  • Customizability: Allow children to modify text, emojis, and style of the sticker.
  • Portability: Print and share options reinforce physical ownership and emotional reflection.

Design Principles & Pedagogical Insights

  • Curiosity: Encourage playful exploration and acceptance of imperfection.
  • Ownership: Prioritize agency—children control how AI outputs represent their feelings.
  • Enjoyment: Humor and surprise transform emotional discomfort into connection:contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}.

These principles align with the Pedagogy of Play framework (Harvard Project Zero), which emphasizes curiosity, ownership, and enjoyment as drivers of engagement and resilience. I extend this framework into emotional interaction design—positioning AI as a creative partner, not an authority.

Outcome & Reflection

The AI Emotional Sticker Tool demonstrated how play can mediate emotion learning through co-creation and humor. The final design empowers children to externalize emotions, compare perspectives, and develop empathy through shared artifacts.

  • 60+ emotional stickers and 8 empathy games co-created by participants.
  • New UX framework: “Curiosity–Ownership–Enjoyment” for emotionally intelligent design.
  • Validated participatory AI approach applicable to future HCI and educational tools.

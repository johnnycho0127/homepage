UX Case Study

Emotions at Play: Designing Child-Centered AI for Emotional Learning

A participatory UX research and design project exploring how AI and tangible play can foster emotional intelligence in children. I led the full design cycle — from generative research and prototyping in Figma to iterative testing with real users.

Design Philosophy

My design philosophy centers on emotionally intelligent design — experiences that cultivate empathy, agency, and joy. I believe design should not only solve functional problems but also nurture emotional and social growth.

Rooted in participatory design and constructivist learning, my work treats users (in this case, children) as creative collaborators. Play becomes a medium for co-design, and technology — particularly AI — becomes a reflective partner rather than an authority.

UX Methodology

The project followed an iterative UX process integrating user research, ideation, prototyping, and evaluation. Each phase informed the next through reflection and co-creation.

  • Discover: Understand how children naturally express and regulate emotions through play.
  • Define: Identify design opportunities for emotional reflection and empathy building.
  • Design: Prototype tangible and digital tools that externalize emotions safely.
  • Develop: Implement AI interaction flows and user interfaces in Figma.
  • Evaluate: Test prototypes with children and analyze behavioral and emotional engagement.

Discover · User Research

Working with 27 children (ages 6–12), I conducted participatory sessions blending storytelling, empathy games, and reflective play. We captured drawings, emotional narratives, and group interactions to understand how children make sense of emotions.

  • Observed natural emotional expression through storytelling and humor.
  • Documented peer empathy patterns (“me too” moments).
  • Noted preference for tangible over digital-only interactions.

Insight → Play can be a powerful medium for emotional regulation and shared reflection.

Develop · Technical Interaction Design

The AI component was designed with prompt engineering and tone calibration to ensure emotionally safe responses. The interaction architecture includes three flows:

  • Input Flow: Story upload, emotion selection, and contextual description.
  • Generation Flow: AI-generated visual + text reflection.
  • Personalization Flow: Edit, decorate, and print for physical ownership.

Evaluate · User Testing & Iteration

Children interacted with the tool individually and in pairs, generating stickers for themselves and peers. They critiqued AI responses (“too corny!”), adjusted designs, and reflected on emotional meaning.

Their feedback led to refinements in tone, customization, and UI simplicity. Humor and imperfection proved to be powerful engagement drivers.

Reflect · Design Impact

  • Developed a hybrid digital–physical system for emotional learning.
  • Produced over 60 AI-generated emotional artifacts co-created with users.
  • Generated the “Curiosity–Ownership–Enjoyment” design framework for emotional UX.
  • Informed teaching practice for participatory and ethical AI design in UX curriculum.

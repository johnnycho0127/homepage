AI-Emotional Sticker

AI SEL Youth HCI

The Feel-Better Sticker project explores how AI can support children’s emotional reflection and empathy through playful, creative tools. Children ages 6–12 designed digital “feel-better” stickers using their own stories and emotions, blending AI image generation and tangible making. This work is part of the Emotions at Play research series on AI and Social-Emotional Learning (SEL).

Open Live Demo

1. Research Goal

  • Explore how AI tools can facilitate children’s emotional self-expression and peer empathy.
  • Investigate how playful, tangible creation (stickers, drawings) supports emotional understanding.
  • Give children agency in co-creating with AI and reflecting on how technology interprets feelings.

2. Design Process

Feel-Better Sticker (Self)
  1. Draw a feeling story (sad, angry, scared, worried, lonely).
  2. Upload the drawing + description to the web tool.
  3. AI generates a sticker and supportive message.
  4. Regenerate/refine (optional); then print & personalize.
Feel-Better Sticker (Peer)
  1. Listen to a peer’s story, generate a sticker + message for them.
  2. Compare self vs. peer designs; discuss empathy & perspectives.

3. Technical Overview

Web-based prototype (React/Node.js on Replit). Children upload drawings + emotion text; the app generates a supportive message and a sticker image with AI, with repeated safety checks.

Pipeline
  • Input: photo of activity sheet, selected emotion, text description.
  • Message: generated by an LLM based on the child’s inputs.
  • Image: sticker created from the model’s prompt via a text-to-image model.
  • Safety: automated checks at multiple steps.
Regeneration

Children may add extra instructions to regenerate the sticker and/or message; the system updates the prompt and re-renders accordingly.

4. Findings

  • Ownership & joy: Kids took pride—“I like this one! Can we make another one?!”
  • Creativity as coping: Humor/play reframed discomfort.
  • Agency through design: AI as a partner (e.g., “I wanted a ton of dogs on it”).

5. Research Insights

  • Playful emotional learning: AI as mirror and mediator of feelings.
  • Empathy through co-creation: Making for peers deepened understanding.
  • “Campfire effect”: Group sharing fostered safety and curiosity.

6. Reflection

“As the lead researcher, I co-designed the AI tool and study activities, facilitated children’s sessions, and analyzed how technology mediates emotional meaning-making. This work informs my ongoing research on AI-supported emotional learning and child-centered digital well-being.”

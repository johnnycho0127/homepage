The Feel-Better Sticker

This project explores how AI can support children’s emotional reflection and empathy through playful, creative tools. Children ages 6–12 designed digital “feel-better” stickers using their own stories and emotions, blending AI image generation and tangible making.

View Live Demo

1. Research Goal

  • To explore how AI tools can facilitate children’s emotional self-expression and peer empathy.
  • To investigate how playful, tangible creation (stickers, drawings) supports emotional understanding.
  • To give children agency in co-creating with AI and reflecting on how technology interprets feelings.

2. Design Process

Feel-Better Sticker (Self)

Children created stickers about their own emotional experiences through a guided workflow:

  1. Draw a feeling story: Illustrate a real emotional moment (sad, angry, scared, worried, lonely).
  2. Share with AI: Upload the drawing and emotion description into a web-based AI tool.
  3. Generate sticker & message: AI produced a visual sticker and a short supportive message.
  4. Regenerate & refine (optional): Children could adjust the image or message.
  5. Print & personalize: Final stickers were printed and decorated for sharing or keeping.

Feel-Better Sticker (Peer)

To foster empathy, children also created stickers for each other. Each child listened to a peer’s story and generated a sticker and message for that friend. They then compared their designs (self vs. peer) and discussed differences, sparking reflection on empathy and perspective-taking.

3. Technical Overview

The AI sticker tool was a web-based prototype built on Replit using React/Node.js. It interacted with several AI models via APIs.

  1. Input Upload: Child uploads activity-sheet photo, selects emotion, adds a brief description.
  2. Initial Content Generation:
    • LLM: Generates a supportive message, emotion description, and an image prompt.
    • Image model: Produces the sticker from the prompt.
    • Safety checks: Automated moderation at multiple steps.
  3. Display: Shows sticker, message, and emotion description.
  4. Regeneration (Optional): Child can add instructions to refine the output.
  5. Display Regenerated Content: Page updates with the new sticker/message.

4. Findings

Ownership & Joy

Children expressed pride in making something uniquely theirs, often asking, “Can we make another one!?”

Creativity as Coping

Humor and playful AI mistakes helped reframe emotional discomfort into a fun, creative activity.

Agency Through Design

Kids customized stickers with favorite elements, showing AI as a partner in creativity.

5. Research Insights

  • Playful Emotional Learning: AI can act as a mirror and mediator of feelings.
  • Empathy through Co-creation: Making for peers deepened understanding.
  • The Campfire Effect: Group sharing fostered emotional safety and curiosity.

6. My Role & Reflection

"As the lead researcher, I co-designed the AI tool and study activities, facilitated children’s sessions, and analyzed how technology mediates emotional meaning-making. This work informs my ongoing research on AI-supported emotional learning and child-centered digital well-being."

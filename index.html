About Me

I am a final-year PhD candidate at the University of Washington's Information School, advised by Dr. Jin Ha Lee. My dissertation focuses on "Misinformation and Teenagers," where I explore the role of Social and Emotional Learning in enhancing Media and Information Literacy.

My academic journey includes an M.S.Ed. from the University of Pennsylvania, where I researched AR-based learning, and an M.A. in Media Studies from Syracuse University, focusing on HCI and immersive technology. My background in Media and Communication Technology from Michigan State University provides a strong technical foundation for my work. I am a U.S. Permanent Resident (Green Card holder) authorized to work in the United States.

Research Vision

My research aims to empower young people to navigate the complex digital world with resilience and critical awareness. I investigate how immersive and interactive technologies, combined with social-emotional learning principles, can create effective educational interventions against misinformation. I believe in designing tools and experiences that not only teach critical evaluation but also foster the emotional intelligence needed to engage with information responsibly.
